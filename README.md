# Yuvaan Mars Rover — Mechanical & Electronics Infrastructure

This repository hosts the core software, control frameworks, and hardware architecture documentation for **Yuvaan**, an advanced planetary exploration rover engineered in accordance with the International Rover Challenge (IRC) framework. The system features a highly adaptable structural body, multi-axis robotic manipulators, and a modular astrobiology laboratory designed for deep-terrain traversal and automated sample analysis.

---

## 🏗️ Mechanical Architecture & Subsystems

The mechanical framework of Yuvaan prioritizes load-bearing stability, structural endurance, and mission-grade traction across unpredictable, cratered landscapes.

### 1. Chassis & Form Factor
* Built using highly modular, high-strength **Aluminium T-slot extrusions** to optimize strength-to-weight efficiency and ensure structural rigidity under intense load variations.
* Features a dedicated slotted **Aluminium base plate** specifically aligned to support the rapid mounting and stress isolation of the automated astrobiology drilling unit.

### 2. Articulated Suspension & Differential Support
* **Rocker-Bogie Suspension:** Utilizes two independent, rigid angular linkages fabricated from **SS-304 stainless steel** to resist abrasion and structural fatigue while maintaining constant wheel-terrain contact.
* **Bearing Reliability:** Integrates a **Dual-Bearing Support System** consisting of self-aligning pillow-block bearings mounted on a reinforced cross-member. This configuration provides stable two-point support for the main suspension shaft, drastically reducing vibration and shaft deflection.
* **Kinematic Optimization:** Validated to maintain a **2.27 factor of safety** under a nominal 32.5 kg linkage payload, resulting in an engineered design load capacity of $\approx73.8$ kg ($\approx724$ N) under standard gravity. Geometrical tracking of angles $\theta$ and $\phi$ minimizes downward load transfers, preventing subsidence on granular terrain.
* **Differential Bar System:** A lightweight, overhead aluminium differential links the suspension sides, dynamically balancing weight distribution like a mechanical scale to keep the chassis horizontal when passing over slopes, rocks, and craters.

### 3. Actuation, Mobility & End-Effectors
* **High-Traction Wheels:** Employs elastic **Polyurethane (PU) wheels with custom Silicone Rims** designed to absorb impacts, increase surface area contact on loose M-Sand, and seal the internal drive motors/encoders from fine particulate dust.
* **Manipulator Wrist Assembly:** Driven by a robust, custom **bevel-gear system** where dual driven gears pair with driver pinions to enforce high-torque output, holding alignment under heavy load lifting.
* **Dual 4-Bar Linkage Gripper:** Employs specialized parallel jaws featuring a precise pinching tip to pick up, manipulate, and transport tiny or irregularly shaped objects with absolute fidelity.

## ⚡ Electronics, Power Distribution & Control

The embedded power management and control system regulates energy distribution across high-power actuators while maintaining stable signal paths for low-voltage computing devices.

### 1. Hierarchical Processing Layer
* **Primary Compute:** The **NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano** acts as the primary onboard computer for high-level processing, running the vision engine and AI-based mission decision-making.
* **Low-Level Microcontrollers:** High-speed **ESP32 microcontrollers** leverage dual-core LX6 processing capabilities to directly parse real-time encoder counts, generate high-frequency PWM waveforms, and handle low-level motor actuation.

### 2. Power Regulation & Protection Network
* **Main Energy Supply:** Fueled by a massive **22.2V, 25C, 22,000 mAh Li-Po battery pack** configured to sustain extensive, multi-hour field missions.
* **Intelligent Battery Management System (BMS):** Integrates specialized protection and sensing ICs to enforce active cell balancing, over-voltage thresholds, over-current trip lines, and critical thermal management safety routines.
* **Isolated Rails:** Utilizes a network of heavy-duty buck-boost converters and step-down units (such as 7805 IC networks) to isolate sensitive communication modules and computer boards from back-EMF spikes generated by high-current motor arrays.

### 3. Motor Driving & Sensor Interfacing
* **Locomotion Actuators:** Utilizes heavy-duty **DC encoder geared motors** paired with quadrature feedback loops to deliver precise speed and position control across a wide torque range (60 kg-cm to 650 kg-cm).
* **Motor Drivers:** Regulated via high-current **Cytron MDD20A and L298N dual-channel motor drivers** supporting bidirectional PWM-based speed switching.
* **Sensor Bus Infrastructure:** Interfaces an **IMU** for real-time stabilization and orientation tracking, **Wheel Encoders** for continuous motion estimation, and an on-board Antenna for data and command transmission.

## 🛠️ Automated Astrobiology Module

A specialized electromechanical testing unit designed for core soil sample harvesting and real-time chemical assay testing.
* **Auger Drill & Syringe Delivery:** A linear actuator drives an auger drill bit vertically into the soil to collect material. Upon retraction, the drill rotates to release soil tangentially into sample bays, where a software-timed syringe assembly drops chemical reagents into reaction containers.
* **On-Board Diagnostics:** Runs a colorimetric assay pipeline via a multi-beaker carrying plate to check for organic biosignatures indicating potential life indicators, including proteins (Biuret Reagent Test), nitrates (Diphenylamine Nitrate Test), and lipids (Sudan IV Stain Test).
* **Analytical Array:** Utilizes an integrated spectrometer to detect resulting color changes and a USB microscope to record real-time soil texture and fluorescence data.
